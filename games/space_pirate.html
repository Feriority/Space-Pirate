Unknown Station

You almost think you recognize the woman running for the escape pod, but before it clicks, the deck shakes violently again. As the running woman stumbles, you nearly lose your footing, and it occurs to you that you, too, should run to the escape pod. It's the last one in this part of the station, and you won't have time to make it to one of the other decks.

The woman - who IS she? - makes it to the pod before you. You're running as fast as you can, but seconds before you reach the pod, you hear the hiss of the door starting to close, and see her mouth "sorry".

You dive for the door, but it's too late - the door slams closed with a bang. You're not going to make it off the station - but despite the situation, you laugh softly, as you recognize the pained expression on her face as the door closed, and remember....

The Spyglass

You slowly open your eyes to the sound of someone's voice. As you groggily sit up, the dream slips away. There was a station, and there was... someone else there?

"-up! We're on approach to Artosis Station now. The official salvage team has already been dispatched. You'll need to work quickly if you're going to get anything of value." *Caliburn's voice crackles from the speakers in your room as you get out of bed.

Your ship, the Spyglass, is a small transport craft you've converted mostly into storage space for smuggling, with just enough room for you to live comfortably. It might be held together by duct tape and nanowire, but *Caliburn, the experimental AI you... liberated from a research lab on Klein, has turned out to be a godsend. When it isn't ruining your attempts to get some sleep, anyway.

It can pilot, manage the ship's systems, help make plans, and has a lot more personality than a run-of-the-mill shipboard AI, though that comes with a few quirks (like its insistence on the star in its name, an outdated AI naming convention that nobody pronounces anyway.)

You stop reminiscing about that job - the important thing is that *Caliburn is working with you now. "We'll be docking shortly. I would recommend that you get ready, unless you've learned to breathe in hard vacuum." Well, maybe it has a little too much personality.

You slip into your spacesuit like you've done a thousand times before. It gives you some protection from the environmental hazards of a derelict space station - most notably, the lack of breathable air you'll almost certainly encounter, but it also regulates temperature well enough to handle most of what you could run into, and even cushions a little against heavy impacts, which has saved your life before.

Once you've got the suit on, you lock the helmet in place. Diagnostics flash across your visor as its HUD boots up and tests your suit, then a handful of condition monitors - for you and the suit - appear in a corner, out of the way of your vision.

"I'm uploading a map of the station to your HUD." *Caliburn's voice comes over the speakers in your helmet, rather than from the ship; the helmet should allow you to communicate with him as you explore the station. The map blinks in front of you, then hides itself in another corner of your view.

You're almost ready to go. Like you always do, you reach for...

  • your weapon - it could be dangerous.
  • your tool kit - how else are you going to loot the ship?

your gun, obviously. The last time you didn't take a gun with you on a job, you were ambushed by someone else who wanted a chance at the same loot you did, and you were lucky to get away with your life. Since then, you've gotten very good with your pistol - it's powerful enough to make a difference, but small enough that it doesn't slow you down or get in your way, and it's easy to hide.

your tool kit, obviously. The suit might have some basic equipment, but your tools are more than just the minimum for repairs. You have a variety of useful gadgets, collected over the years, and you're always on the lookout for another trick that might be the difference between success and failure - or even life and death.

As you equip yourself, the Spyglass is pulling up to the station, a rotating ring around a central spire - a common design for old stations, or newer ones being built cheaply. The outer wall becomes the floor, using the station's rotation to simulate gravity. Sections of the ring's wall are crumpled, and others are missing entirely. It's surprising that the station is still spinning, or holding up as well as it is, considering the structural damage.

"Docking the ship now. Brace for impact." The Spyglass shudders as *Caliburn docks. All that's left is to leave the ship and get to the station.

Artosis Station

You enter the station through a breach in the hull. Some of the interior bulkheads have been blown out, but others are locked down from the station's attempt to contain the damage during evacuation, which will limit your ability to move around the station unless you can find a way to deal with it.

"I'm annotating your map with directions. The spinning of the station gives us our reference - the direction it rotates is turnwise, and the other direction is anti-turnwise." *Caliburn updates your map accordingly.

You find yourself in a residential area of the station. There are a number of cabins nearby, which are easy enough for you to break into; you look for anything worth looting, but the real valuables are going to be elsewhere in the station, and you don't find anything worth taking.

You're in the residential area you used to enter the station. The Spyglass is anchored to the station's hull outside a breach. There are a number of cabins, which you have already searched.

There are doors turnwise and anti-turnwise to other parts of the station. According to your map, they lead to an off-duty area and the main labs, respectively.

This part of the station was largely for off-duty crew - the mess hall, a large recreation area, and some areas for relaxation. Your map suggests there was a library where there is now a sealed bulkhead and a large hole. There should be storage areas here, exactly what you were looking for, but a breach in one of them and a blown out bulkhead have caused the path to them to be locked down. You think you can see a way to one of the gyms, and the map shows a path to the central spire. The command deck should be turnwise from here.

You can also go back to the entrance.

The gym is in pretty bad shape, and it's not like you're going to steal broken exercise equipment. Still, you take a quick look around.

This part of the station was where the crew worked. Most of it is lab space, but you don't have the time to move the heavy equipment that would be most valuable; you'll have to settle for the various samples and small things you grabbed during a quick pass.

Your map shows a medbay, a security office, and a path to the central spire. The command deck should be anti-turnwise from here.

You can also go back to the entrance.

The station's medbay is well equipped - the small research team here had to be self-sufficient for long periods at a time. You wish you could take some of the larger equipment back with you.

With the security systems disabled, you find it easy to make it into the security offices. Whatever happened here, they got hit pretty badly - the area is mostly destroyed. Strangely, some of the blown out walls seem to have been destroyed from the inside.

Normally, this would be a great location to loot, but the armory seems to have been lost to the vacuum.

You make your way through the command deck slowly and carefully. Rubble is everywhere, but oddly the hull doesn't appear to have been breached anywhere in this section of the station; it's just the interior structures that have been blasted apart.

A large, transparent section of the hull allows you to see the planet spinning below. The view is amazing; for a moment, you remember exactly why you decided to spend your life exploring space instead of settling down on some station or colony. There's some sort of storm raging below; it must be huge, to be visible from orbit, and the whirling red and purple clouds are a fantastic sight.

You try to reach the actual station controls and communication systems, but they're completely inaccessible. You can, however, access the overrides to the doors that lead turnwise and anti-turnwise, which should give you a clear path around the entire loop of the station.

There is another path to the spire from this deck. You can also go turnwise to the main labs or anti-turnwise to the off-duty area.

This path to the spire, at least, is totally clear of rubble. You find a gate blocking off the elevator into the core of the station, however - the warnings indicate that the spire labs are high security, and under lockdown. There's also various safety warnings about the shifting gravity along the elevator path, and the low-gravity spire.

The elevator to the spire is unpowered; you have to climb a ladder up an emergency shaft. It's long, but the gravity drops off as you climb, so it's an easier trip than it looks. You're practically flying at the end of the climb, pushing off the ladder to move up the shaft.

There's a second sealed door at the top of the shaft, but your security card unlocks it. You float through, and find yourself in another lab. This one is a wreck; the spire walls are perforated, despite looking a a lot sturdier than the ring walls were. Most of the equipment is in ruins.

"Hey, boss?" *Caliburn cuts through. "We've got movement at the outer comm buoy. I hope you've found something good, because we need to get out of here fast."

You don't have much time, but your findings have been pretty meager so far - you should at least search this lab before you leave. It's what it looked like, all wreckage - but then you move what's left of a door aside, and find a brilliant glowing ball, floating in place. It's surface is covered in violently shifting reds and purples like the storm you saw on the planet below.

You consider trying to grab the orb, but something about it feels wrong. It would be smarter to leave it alone and go back.

You don't really remember how you made it back to the Spyglass, but you get aboard in time and detach the ship from Artosis Station's hull. By the time you're ready to leave, you can already see the Euryphaessa approaching. It's a huge warship - not what you'd expect to see here at all. You briefly wonder why it's here, but know better than to stick around to find out - you get out of there, quick.

As you jump out of the system, you get the feeling you've forgotten about something important...

End of Act 1
