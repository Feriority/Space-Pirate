Unknown Station

You almost think you recognize the woman running for the escape pod, but before it clicks, the deck shakes violently again. The woman stumbles, you nearly lose your footing, and it occurs to you that you, too, should run to the escape pod. It's the last one in this area - most of the crew is already gone.

The woman - who IS she? - makes it to the pod before you. You run as fast as you can to catch up, but seconds before you reach your last chance to escape, you hear the hiss of the door closing. In the moment it takes to shut, you can see her mouth "sorry".

Then the moment is gone and the door slams closed with a bang. You're not going to make it off the station - but you laugh, as you recognize that last expression, and you remember...

The Spyglass

As you groggily get up, the dream slips away. You recall something about a station, and there was someone else there?

"-up! We're on approach to the station now. The salvage team isn't here yet - if we're quick, we can grab something good before they show up." Your little ship might be held together by duct tape and string, but the experimental AI you... liberated, *Caliburn, has turned out to be a godsend. When it isn't ruining your attempts to get some sleep, anyway.

Well, you'd better get ready, if you're about to dock. Your gear gives you some protection from the environmental hazards of a derelict space station - most notably, the lack of breathable air you'll almostly certainly encounter. Naturally, you also reach for...

  • your weapon - it could be dangerous.
  • your tools - how else are you going to loot the ship?

your gun, obviously. Sure, parts of the station might be exposed to vacuum, but not all of it is, and you don't know what happened to it in the first place. Do you really want to go in there unarmed?

your tools, obviously. Yeah, there'll be some stuff you can just walk out with, but having the right tools gives you a lot more options.

As you equip yourself, the Spyglass is pulling up to the station. Your AI has found a good spot to get you through the hull. All that's left is to put on your helmet and get to the station.

In addition to giving you feedback on your suit's condition, the helmet's HUD shows you some navigation information and allows you to communicate with *Caliburn while away from your ship.

The station is a rotating ring around a central spire. This gives some natural references for navigation - moving along with the station is turnwise, and moving against it is anti-turnwise. The floor is the outer wall of the ring, to take advantage of the simulated gravity the rotation creates.

Artosis Station

You enter the station through a breach in the hull. Some of the interior bulkheads have been blown out, but others are locked down from the station's attempt to contain the damage during evacuation, which will limit your ability to move around the station unless you can find a way to deal with it.

You find yourself in a residential area of the station. There are a number of cabins nearby, which are easy enough for you to break into; you look for anything worth looting, but the real valuables are going to be elsewhere in the station, and you don't find anything worth taking.

You're in the residential area you used to enter the station. The Spyglass is anchored to the station's hull outside a breach. There are a number of cabins, which you have already searched.

There are doors turnwise and anti-turnwise to other parts of the station. According to your map, they lead to an off-duty area and the main labs, respectively.

This part of the station was largely for off-duty crew - the mess hall, a large recreation area, and some areas for relaxation. A map on the wall suggests there was a library where there is now a sealed bulkhead and a large hole. There should be storage areas here, exactly what you were looking for, but a breach in one of them and a blown out bulkhead have caused the path to them to be locked down. You think you can see a way to one of the gyms, and the map shows a path to the central spire. The command deck should be turnwise from here.

You can also go back to the entrance.

Nothing here yet, go back

This part of the station was where the crew worked. Most of it is lab space, but you don't have the time to move the heavy equipment that would be most valuable; you'll have to settle for the various samples and small things you grabbed during a quick pass.

The map on the wall shows a medbay, a security office, and a path to the central spire. The command deck should be anti-turnwise from here.

You can also go back to the entrance.

Nothing here yet, go back

With the security systems disabled, you find it easy to make it into the security offices. Whatever happened here, they got hit pretty badly - the area is mostly destroyed. Strangely, some of the blown out walls seem to have been destroyed from the inside.

Normally, this would be a great location to loot, but the armory seems to have been lost to the vacuum.

You make your way through the command deck slowly and carefully. Rubble is everywhere, but oddly the hull doesn't appear to have been breached anywhere in this section of the station; it's just the interior structures that have been blasted apart.

A large, transparent section of the hull allows you to see the planet spinning below. The view is amazing; for a moment, you remember exactly why you decided to spend your life as a spacer instead of settling down on some station or colony. There's some sort of storm raging below; it must be huge, to be visible from orbit, and the whirling red and purple clouds are a fantastic sight.

You try to reach the actual station controls and communication systems, but they're completely inaccessible. You can, however, access the overrides to the doors that lead turnwise and anti-turnwise, which should give you a clear path around the entire loop of the station.

There is another path to the spire from this deck. You can also go turnwise to the main labs or anti-turnwise to the off-duty area.

This path to the spire, at least, is totally clear of rubble. You find a gate blocking off the elevator into the core of the station, however - the warnings indicate that the spire labs are high security, and under lockdown. There's also various safety warnings about the shifting gravity along the elevator path, and the low-gravity spire.

The elevator to the spire is unpowered; you have to climb a ladder up an emergency shaft. It's long, but the gravity drops off as you climb, so it's an easier trip than it looks. You're practically flying at the end of the climb, pushing off the ladder to move up the shaft.

There's a second sealed door at the top of the shaft, but your security card unlocks it. You float through, and find yourself in another lab. This one is a wreck; the spire walls are perforated, despite looking a a lot sturdier than the ring walls were. Most of the equipment is in ruins.

"Hey, boss?" *Caliburn cuts through. "We've got movement at the outer comm buoy. I hope you've found something good, because we need to get out of here fast."

You don't have much time, but your findings have been pretty meager so far - you should at least search this lab before you leave. It's what it looked like, all wreckage - but then you move what's left of a door aside, and find a brilliant glowing ball, floating in place. It's surface is covered in violently shifting reds and purples like the storm you saw on the planet below.

You consider trying to grab the orb, but something about it feels wrong. It would be smarter to leave it alone and go back.

You don't really remember how you made it back to the Spyglass, but you get aboard in time and detach the ship from Artosis Station's hull. By the time you're ready to leave, you can already see the Euryphaessa approaching. It's a huge warship - not what you'd expect to see here at all. You briefly wonder why it's here, but know better than to stick around to find out - you get out of there, quick.

As you jump out of the system, you get the feeling you've forgotten about something important...

End of Act 1
